Can I spray roof maxx myself?

5 Questions Answered

Can I spray roof maxx myself?

No. Roof Maxx is a dealer-exclusive product, meaning it is only available to certified Roof Maxx dealers and is not sold to the general public for DIY application. Proper application requires specialized equipment and training to ensure even coverage and product effectiveness. Applying it incorrectly can void any treatment warranty. Homeowners must hire an authorized Roof Maxx dealer to have the treatment professionally applied to their asphalt shingles.

What is the alternative to roof Maxx?

Roof coating alternatives to Roof Maxx are products or treatments designed to extend asphalt shingle life or protect roofing surfaces through rejuvenation or protective coatings. Common alternatives include asphalt rejuvenators like Reclaim, elastomeric roof coatings for flat or low-slope roofs, and acrylic or silicone spray coatings. Some homeowners also opt for traditional roof replacement when shingles are beyond treatment. The right alternative depends on roof age, shingle condition, and the specific goals of the property owner. Learn more about roof repair alternatives and options.

How much does roof Maxx charge to spray your roof?

$150 to $1,500 or more, depending on roof size, pitch, and location. Three key factors affect the final price: (1) **Roof square footage** — larger roofs require more product and labor; (2) **Roof complexity** — steep or multi-faceted roofs take longer to treat safely; (3) **Local market rates** — pricing in the Godfrey, IL area may differ from national averages based on regional dealer pricing structures. Most homeowners report costs significantly lower than full roof replacement.

Do insurance companies accept roof Maxx?

It depends. Some insurance companies acknowledge Roof Maxx treatments favorably because the product can extend shingle flexibility and reduce deterioration, potentially lowering claim risk. However, insurance acceptance varies widely by provider and policy terms. Roof Maxx does offer a five-year transferable warranty, which can support conversations with insurers. Homeowners should contact their insurance provider directly to confirm whether a Roof Maxx treatment affects their coverage, premium, or roof age assessment before scheduling service.

How long does roof Max spray last?

A single Roof Maxx application is designed to last approximately five years, with the potential to extend overall shingle life by up to 15 years through three sequential treatments. Effectiveness depends on the current condition of the shingles, climate exposure, and roof maintenance practices. Shingles that are severely damaged or past their useful life may not respond as well. Trill Roofing recommends having a professional assess shingle condition before treatment to determine whether the roof is a good candidate.
