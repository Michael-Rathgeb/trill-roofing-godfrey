What is the 25% rule in roofing?

The 25% rule states that if more than 25% of a roof's surface is damaged or deteriorated, full replacement is recommended rather than repair. Illinois building codes use this threshold to determine permit requirements. For example, on a 2,000 sq ft Roxana home, if 500+ square feet shows compromised shingles, replacement becomes the practical choice. This guideline helps Roxana homeowners make informed decisions about whether to repair or replace aging roofs.

Will homeowners insurance cover roof repair?

Coverage depends on damage cause. Homeowners insurance in Illinois typically covers repairs from sudden events like storms, hail, wind, fire, or fallen trees. It generally excludes damage from age, wear and tear, neglect, or poor maintenance. Roxana homeowners should know that older roofs may be covered at actual cash value rather than replacement cost, factoring in depreciation. Review your specific policy to understand what damage types your coverage includes.
